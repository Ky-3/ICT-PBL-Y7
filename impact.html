The Ripple Effect: Nga Moe Yate Creek

As Yangon’s primary industrial and urban artery, the Nga Moe Yate Creek faces a critical "triple threat" of pollution that impacts our environment, our health, and our economy.

01

Biological Suffocation

Excess nitrogen and phosphorus from urban runoff cause Eutrophication. This triggers massive algae blooms that strip oxygen from the NgaMoeYeik Creek, creating "dead zones" where fish and aquatic plants can no longer survive.

02

Chemical Toxicity

Industrial discharges from factories along the riverbanks introduce heavy metals like lead and mercury. These micropollutants don't just stay in the water; they accumulate in the food chain, affecting both wildlife and the residents who consume river fish.

03

The Human Toll

For the thousands living in Yangon's riverside wards, the NgaMoeYeik Creek is a daily health risk. Contaminated water leads to a high prevalence of waterborne diseases, placing a constant burden on local healthcare facilities and family livelihoods.

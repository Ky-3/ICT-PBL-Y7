Water Security in Yangon

Yangon is a city deeply dependent on its water, from the NgaMoeYeik Creek to the small lakes in our wards. However, this vital resource is currently under threat.

In 2026, water pollution in our city has become one of our biggest challenges. It occurs when harmful substances, such as industrial chemicals, plastic waste, and bacteria contaminate our rivers and groundwater. As the city grows rapidly, the volume of waste rises, making water toxic for both humans and our local nature. Protecting our water is not just an environmental goal; it is essential for the city’s future health and prosperity.
